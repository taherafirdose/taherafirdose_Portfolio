Price Optimization Using Machine Learning Techniques

Overview: This project focuses on utilizing Machine Learning (ML) techniques to optimize pricing strategies in a café business scenario. By analyzing a rich dataset with sales information, the project aims to understand how various factors like date, item prices, quantities sold, and special conditions (holidays, weekends, etc.) influence sales. The primary goal is to determine optimal pricing for different products under varying circumstances, ultimately enhancing profitability and customer satisfaction.

Objectives:

  • To analyze sales data of a café and understand the impact of different variables on product sales.
  • To apply Machine Learning models, including Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression and Expectile Generalized Additive Models (GAMs), to predict the effects of pricing on sales quantities.
  • To identify optimal pricing strategies for different products (Burgers, Coke, Lemonade, and Coffee) considering various factors like holidays, weekends, and seasonal trends.
  • To provide actionable insights for price adjustments to maximize revenue without compromising customer demand.

Data Analysis:

  • The dataset includes variables like date, price, quantity, seller ID, seller category, item type, year, holiday status, weekend status, school break, average temperature, and outdoor sales.
  • Initial data cleaning includes handling missing values and adding new columns for better analysis (e.g., ‘is_event’, ‘revenue’).
  • Detailed exploratory data analysis to understand sales patterns, item popularity, and the impact of external factors like holidays and weather conditions on sales.
  • Comparative analysis of sales during different conditions (e.g., holidays vs. non-holidays, weekends vs. weekdays).

Methodology:

  • Application of OLS regression to understand the influence of various factors on the quantity sold.
  • Utilization of Expectile GAMs to examine the relationship between price and quantity sold at different quantiles (2.5th, 50th, 97.5th percentiles), allowing for a nuanced understanding of price sensitivity and demand elasticity.
  • Predictive modeling for price optimization of different items, considering various scenarios like non-holiday periods and specific holidays.
  • Identification of optimal pricing points for each item across different conditions to maximize revenue.

Key Findings:

  • Insights into the price sensitivity for different items, revealing unique demand patterns for each product.
  • Identification of optimal price points for various items during regular and special days.
  • Understanding of how sales quantities vary with different pricing strategies and external factors like holidays.

Conclusion: This project illustrates the power of machine learning in transforming traditional price optimization strategies. By employing advanced data analytics and ML models, businesses can gain a deeper understanding of their sales dynamics and customer behavior. These insights enable the formulation of data-driven pricing strategies that not only enhance profitability but also ensure adaptability in a constantly evolving market.

Tools and Technologies Used:

Data Analysis: Python, Pandas, NumPy Machine Learning Models: OLS Regression, Expectile GAMs Data Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn Potential Impact: This project highlights how data-driven approaches can revolutionize pricing strategies in the retail and hospitality sectors. It serves as a blueprint for businesses looking to leverage machine learning for enhanced decision-making, offering a competitive edge in today’s data-centric business landscape.
